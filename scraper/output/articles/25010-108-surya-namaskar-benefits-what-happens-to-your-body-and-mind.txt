108 Surya Namaskar Benefits: What Happens to Your Body and Mind

108 Surya Namaskar means completing 108 consecutive rounds of the Sun Salutation sequence — twelve postures coordinated with breath. A single session works every major muscle group, trains the cardiovascular system, and builds the focused calm that carries into the rest of your day. Practised daily over 30 days, the cumulative effect on flexibility, strength, and mental clarity is significant.

Whether you are exploring 
Surya Namaskara
 for the first time or looking to understand what a sustained daily practice can do, this guide covers the real, evidence-backed benefits — and the honest realities — of committing to 
108 rounds
.

10 Key Benefits of 108 Surya Namaskar

Improves Full-Body Flexibility
Each round takes your spine through forward folds, backbends, and lateral stretches. Over 108 repetitions, tight hamstrings, hip flexors, and shoulders gradually soften. Many people practising daily over 30 days notice a meaningful improvement in overall range of motion.

Supports Cardiovascular Health
Moving through 108 rounds at a steady pace elevates your heart rate in a sustained, low-impact way — comparable to a brisk cardio session. This supports circulation and aerobic endurance over time and complements your existing heart-health routine.

Builds Functional Strength
Chaturanga, Plank, and Downward Dog — repeated across 108 cycles — progressively load your arms, core, and legs without any equipment. Weeks of consistent practice produce noticeable improvements in muscular endurance across the whole body.

Aids Gradual Weight Management
A 45–60 minute session of 108 sun salutations burns a significant number of calories — estimates vary by body weight and pace, but output can rival moderate cardio. Combined with a balanced diet, this supports weight management through consistent practice.

Regulates Breathing Patterns
Every transition in Surya Namaskar is tied to an inhale or exhale. Over 108 rounds, this trains the nervous system toward slower, more controlled breathing — which helps manage the physiological stress response throughout the day.

Enhances Mental Focus and Clarity
Counting 108 repetitions while coordinating breath and movement demands sustained attention. This meditative quality gradually reduces mental scatter and may support management of day-to-day anxiety when practised regularly.

Strengthens Core Stability
The Plank and Chaturanga positions recur every single round. By the end of 108 cycles, your deep abdominal muscles — not just the surface layer — have done considerable work, building the kind of core strength that supports posture and everyday movement.

Improves Posture and Spinal Alignment
Alternating forward folds and backbends counteract the postural damage of long hours at a desk. Practised daily, this sequence gradually eases the forward rounding of the upper back that many working professionals develop over time.

Supports Better Sleep Quality
The combination of physical exertion and breath-led movement tends to lower cortisol. Practitioners who complete 108 rounds in the morning often report sleeping more soundly at night over the course of a 30-day challenge.

Builds Consistent Daily Discipline
Completing 108 rounds — especially in the early days — requires showing up when motivation is low. This is what makes the practice transformative beyond the physical. The discipline built here consistently carries over into other areas of life.

How to Get Started with 108 Surya Namaskar

What You Need to Begin

You need a yoga mat, comfortable clothing, and enough floor space to extend your arms forward and back. No weights, no gym membership, no props. Early morning on an empty stomach is traditional, but any consistent time slot will produce results over 30 days.

Setting Realistic Goals

If you have never practised Surya Namaskar before, start with 
12 rounds
 daily for the first week and build gradually. Jumping to 108 on day one significantly raises injury risk. Aim to reach 
108 rounds
 by week three or four — progress at a pace your body can sustain.

Start with the Basics

Before attempting high volumes, confirm your form in each of the twelve positions is correct. Poor alignment repeated 
108 times
 compounds into strain rather than benefit. Focus on breath first — if you lose the breath-movement connection, slow down rather than push through. 
Guided yoga for beginners
 is the safest entry point if you are new to the sequence.

Best Poses Within the 108 Surya Namaskar Sequence

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

The starting and ending position of each round. Standing tall with feet together and arms by your sides, Tadasana trains you to root into the ground before moving. Inhale as you raise your arms overhead to begin the sequence.

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

One of the most repeated positions in the sequence. Downward Dog lengthens the hamstrings, decompresses the spine, and builds shoulder endurance. Press firmly through your palms and exhale as you draw your hips up and back.

Virabhadrasana (Warrior Pose)

Some Surya Namaskar variations include a low lunge or Warrior transition that opens the hip flexors and builds leg strength. Inhale as you step the foot forward and lift your chest, keeping the front knee stacked directly over the ankle.

Balasana (Child’s Pose)

Not part of the standard twelve-step sequence, but an essential recovery pose to take between sets when practising 
108 rounds
. It allows the lower back to decompress and the breath to settle. Hold for three to five slow breaths before continuing.

Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

One of the most important backbends in the sequence. Bhujangasana strengthens the erector muscles along the spine, opens the chest, and gradually eases upper back tension through consistent practice. Inhale as you peel the chest off the floor, keeping elbows slightly bent and shoulders away from the ears.

Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Salutation)

In classical Surya Namaskar, this position precedes Cobra. The chin, chest, and knees touch the floor while the hips remain raised. It builds chest and tricep endurance — qualities that become very apparent after 50-plus rounds.

Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

The gentle backbend with arms raised overhead opens the chest and stretches the abdominals. Inhale deeply into this position at the start and end of each round to reset the breath and prepare for the next cycle.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping Warm-Up
Starting 108 rounds without warming up the wrists, shoulders, and ankles is one of the most common causes of soreness and minor injury. Spend five to ten minutes on joint rotations and light stretches before your first round — non-negotiable in the first two weeks.

Holding Your Breath During Poses
When the rounds get difficult — usually around the 60–70 mark — many practitioners unconsciously hold their breath. This increases tension and counteracts the calming effect of the practice. If you notice it happening, reduce pace immediately and let the breath lead movement.

Forcing Into Advanced Poses Too Soon
Forcing a deep backbend in Cobra or collapsing the lower back in Downward Dog to get through rounds faster creates more harm than benefit. Quality of movement over 108 rounds matters far more than speed. Reduce range of motion rather than compromise form.

Inconsistent Practice
The most common reason people do not see results from a 108 Surya Namaskar challenge is irregular attendance. Doing 108 rounds once a week produces none of the compounding benefit that daily repetition delivers. Consistency is the only variable that cannot be shortcut.

Who Should Try 108 Surya Namaskar?

Beginners
Absolute beginners should start with 12 rounds daily and build over four to six weeks. The sequence is learnable and approachable — the main requirement is consistency and the willingness to go slowly while form develops.

Women
108 Surya Namaskar is particularly useful for women managing stress, hormonal fluctuations, and the physical effects of a sedentary work routine. The breath-led movement supports the parasympathetic nervous system and may help with mood stability when practised regularly. Note: it is generally advised to reduce intensity or pause during heavy flow days of the menstrual cycle.

Older Adults
For those over 50, a modified Surya Namaskar — with knees slightly bent in forward folds and a gentler Cobra rather than full Upward Dog — is a safe and effective way to maintain joint mobility and muscular endurance. Always check with a healthcare provider before beginning any new physical practice if you have existing joint or cardiovascular conditions.

Working Professionals
For those who spend most of the day seated, 108 Surya Namaskar delivers postural correction, stress support, and cardiovascular stimulus in a single morning session. The structured daily format also builds a reliable routine around an often unpredictable schedule.

Build a Consistent Practice with a Routine That Actually Works

FAQs About 108 Surya Namaskar

What is 108 Surya Namaskar?

108 Surya Namaskar refers to completing 108 consecutive rounds of the Sun Salutation sequence — a flowing series of twelve yoga postures coordinated with breath. The number 108 holds significance in Hindu and yogic traditions and is commonly used as a challenge format for festivals like Makar Sankranti or personal 30-day practice goals.

Is 108 Surya Namaskar good for beginners?

The full 108-round format is not recommended for absolute beginners on day one. Start with 
12 rounds
 and add more each week as strength and endurance build. With a gradual progression plan, most people can comfortably reach 
108 rounds
 within three to four weeks of consistent daily practice.

How often should I practise 108 Surya Namaskar?

For a 30-day challenge, daily practice is ideal. Once the challenge is complete, three to five days a week at 
108 rounds
 — with lighter sessions on the remaining days — is a sustainable long-term frequency. Daily practice produces the most visible results in flexibility and endurance.

Can I do 108 Surya Namaskar at home?

Yes, and most practitioners do. All you need is a yoga mat and enough space to extend your body fully in both directions. Removing the commute to a studio significantly increases the likelihood of showing up daily. 
Yoga classes at home
 with live guidance make this even more effective.

Do I need any equipment for 108 Surya Namaskar?

No equipment is needed. A non-slip yoga mat is strongly recommended to prevent slipping in Downward Dog and Plank. Some practitioners use a towel after the 50-round mark. That is genuinely all you need.

How long before I see results from 108 Surya Namaskar?

Most people practising 108 Surya Namaskar daily report noticeable changes in flexibility and stamina within two weeks. Visible changes in body composition and posture tend to emerge more clearly between weeks three and four of consistent daily practice. Results vary based on diet, sleep, and prior fitness level — the key variable is consistency, not intensity.
