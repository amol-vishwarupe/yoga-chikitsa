Ushas Mudra — the Dawn Gesture — is a yogic hand gesture invoking the energy of Ushas, the Vedic goddess of the dawn, who represents the awakening of consciousness, the renewal of energy, and the clear, fresh awareness of the new day. Formed by interlacing the fingers with the thumbs touching, it activates the Svadhisthana (sacral) chakra, energises the body and mind upon waking, and is traditionally practised as the very first act of the morning — before rising from bed.

What is Ushas Mudra?

Ushas Mudra — pronounced oo-SHAHS moo-DRAH — is named after Ushas, the dawn goddess of the Rigveda who is described as the most beautiful of all celestial beings — the first light that dissolves darkness, awakens the sleeping world, and sets all beings into their daily activity. The mudra invokes this awakening quality: the transition from sleep to wakefulness, from unconscious to conscious, from darkness to light.

The formation: interlace all eight fingers with the right thumb on top (for men; women interlace with the left thumb on top — though contemporary practice often omits this gender distinction). The right thumb presses gently on the left thumb (or the tip of the left thumb presses gently into the base of the right). The interlaced hands rest at the pelvic or navel level. Ushas Mudra specifically activates the Svadhisthana (sacral) chakra — the energy centre of sexuality, creativity, and the flowing, pleasure-oriented engagement with life. How to Do Ushas Mudra — Step-by-Step

Key Principles

Ushas Mudra is most effective when practised immediately upon waking — before rising from bed, before checking the phone, before any morning activity. The traditional instruction is to hold the mudra for 
5–15 minutes
 in the waking-from-sleep state, when the boundary between conscious and unconscious is most permeable and the Svadhisthana activation most potent.

Step 1: Upon Waking — Before Rising
When the morning alarm sounds or natural waking occurs: remain lying or sit gently upright in bed. Do not check the phone or engage with external stimuli. Bring both hands to the navel or pelvic level.

Step 2: Interlace the Fingers
Interlace all eight fingers — right thumb on top for conventional practice, or simply with whichever thumb feels natural. The fingers interlace completely.

Step 3: Apply the Thumb Contact
Bring the right thumb to gently press the left thumbnail or the base of the left thumb. The contact is light and sustained — not forceful.

Step 4: Hold with Dawn Intention
Close or half-close the eyes. Breathe naturally. Cultivate the Ushas intention: the fresh, clear awareness of the new day dawning within and around you. The sacral chakra at the lower abdomen begins to activate with each breath cycle.

Step 5: Hold for 
5–15 Minutes
 Before Rising
Hold throughout the waking transition — 
5 minutes
 for a brief practice, 
15 minutes
 for the complete classical practice. Release the mudra when ready to rise. The morning’s activity begins from this energised, clear state.

Breathing in Ushas Mudra

Natural, awakening breath accompanies Ushas Mudra — the body’s own morning breathing rhythm deepening naturally as the sacral chakra activates. No specific breath ratio or pattern is imposed; the breath simply awakens with the body.

Suryabhedan pranayama (right nostril, solar, warming) can be added after the initial Ushas Mudra hold for the most complete morning energisation practice.
